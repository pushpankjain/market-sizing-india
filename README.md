# Market Sizing - India Consumer Sectors

Structured market sizing frameworks developed during live buy-side and sell-side mandates at PwC, Mumbai. Each analysis below was used to inform investment thesis development, TAM expansion narratives, or IC-level presentations.

---

## How to Size a Market (The Practitioner's Method)

There are two approaches. In practice, good analysis uses both and cross-checks:

**Top-down:** Start with total industry size (from reports like IMARC, Technopak, Euromonitor) → apply addressable segment filters → arrive at TAM.

**Bottom-up:** Start with unit economics (price per unit × purchase frequency × addressable customers) → build upward.

A market size that cannot be arrived at from both directions is suspect.

## Case Study 1 - Men's Ethnic Wear (Kisah Apparels, Buy-side)

### Market Overview

| Parameter | Data Point |
|-----------|-----------|
| Total market size | INR 2.2 Lakh Crore (~USD 26Bn) |
| Growth rate | ~20% CAGR (Technopak estimate) |
| Organised penetration | ~30% of total market |
| Organised market size | ~INR 66,000 Cr. |
| Key organised players | Manyavar, Tasva (Aditya Birla), Kalki |

### The Whitespace Thesis

The ~70% unorganised share represents the core opportunity for branded players - consumers purchasing from local tailors, unbranded wholesalers, or unorganised retail.

**Triggers for organised market share gain:**
- Premiumisation of wedding spending (average ticket sizes rising)
- Growth of large format retail enabling discovery of branded ethnic wear
- Social media driving aspiration for branded occasion wear among Tier 2/3 consumers

### Unit Economics Benchmarking

| Metric | Kisah | Manyavar (est.) | Tasva (est.) |
|--------|-------|-----------------|--------------|
| ASP (Average Selling Price) | Mid-premium | Premium | Ultra-premium |
| Store-level payback | 18–24 months | 12–18 months | 24–30 months |
| Contribution margin profile | Building | Mature | Investing phase |
| CAC/LTV ratio | Early-stage | Established | Early-stage |

*Note: Competitor figures are estimates based on public disclosures and industry benchmarks.*

## Case Study 2 - Ice Cream / Frozen Desserts (Hocco, Buy-side)

### Market Overview

| Parameter | Data Point |
|-----------|-----------|
| Total market size | INR 27,000+ Crore (IMARC estimate) |
| Key organised players | Amul, Kwality Wall's (HUL), Mother Dairy, Naturals |
| Unorganised share | ~35–40% (regional players, local dairy) |
| Premiumisation trend | Strong — consumer shift to natural, artisanal formats |

### Geographic Concentration Risk

Hocco's revenues were concentrated in specific geographies at the time of assessment. For a buy-side follow-on investment decision, we mapped:
- States where brand recall was strong vs. states with negligible presence
- Distribution density (outlets per city) as a proxy for organic growth runway
- Competitive intensity by region (Amul dominates in North; Naturals in West)

### Seasonality Analysis

Ice cream is inherently seasonal - Q1 (Apr–Jun) generates disproportionately high revenues. Key FDD implications:
- Revenue validation must be done on LTM (Last Twelve Months) basis, not a single quarter
- Working capital builds significantly pre-summer and unwinds post-monsoon
- Margin analysis must strip out seasonal distortions before drawing conclusions

## Case Study 3 - D2C Health & Wellness (True Elements, Marico Buy-side)

### Market Context

India's D2C health food segment has expanded rapidly, driven by:
- Post-COVID health consciousness
- Rise of quick commerce enabling D2C-like economics for physical brands
- Social media enabling brand building without traditional retail investment

### Revenue Trajectory Validation

On the True Elements mandate, we stress-tested a claimed **2x revenue trajectory** (INR 76 Cr. → INR 164 Cr. within a year) by:

1. **Channel mix analysis** - What drove the growth? D2C website, Amazon/Flipkart, or modern trade?
2. **Repeat purchase economics** - Are new customers converting to loyal repeat buyers, or is growth driven purely by acquisition spend?
3. **SKU cohort analysis** - Is growth broad-based across the portfolio or driven by 1–2 hero SKUs?
4. **Brand cohesion** - Does the product portfolio feel like one brand, or an incoherent collection of health products?
